Step-by-Step Robot Assembly

Step 1: Arduino Uno

Start by setting up the Arduino Uno as the brain of your robot.

Tip: Ensure the board is powered correctly and drivers are installed.

Step 2: Resistors

Prepare resistors for protecting LEDs, transistors, and other components.

Tip: Use the correct resistance values to avoid damage.

Step 3: Motor Driver

The motor driver is used to control the speed and direction of the DC motors using signals from the Arduino.

Tip: Always connect an external power source for the motors and share a common ground with the Arduino.

Step 4: Car Wheels

They allow the robot to move forward, backward, and turn in different directions.

Tip: Make sure the wheels are firmly fixed to the motor shaft to avoid slipping during movement.

Step 5: Jumper Wires

Connect all components using jumper wires to the Arduino board.

Tip: Keep wires organized to avoid confusion or shorts.

Step 6: Push Buttons

Add push buttons for manual control of the robot.

Tip: Use pull-up or pull-down resistors to prevent floating input.

Step 7: Lithium Batteries

They are lightweight and deliver enough voltage and current to run motors and electronics efficiently.

Tip: Never overcharge or short-circuit lithium batteries, and always use a proper battery holder or protection circuit.

Step 8: Ultrasonic Sensor

Install the ultrasonic sensor for obstacle detection and navigation.

Tip: Position the sensor straight for accurate readings.

Step 9: DC Motors

Connect the DC motors to provide movement to your robot.

Tip: Test each motor separately before full assembly.
